DPT_Projectile Framework

Ammunition & Kinetic Impact Reference Guide
An extensible, production-ready ballistic architecture that handles real-time rendering slerp orientation, matrix-free target locking tracking, continuous Perlin noise aerodynamic drift, and modular sub-mesh fragmentation cleanup.
Quick Navigation Reference: 1. Core Projectile Settings 2. Cannonball Spec 3. Boulder Spec 4. Ballista Bolt Spec 5. Screen Shake & Surface Modules 6. Troubleshooting

1. Projectile Base Class (Abstract)

DPT_Projectile is an abstract MonoBehaviour that governs all ammunition lifecycle routines once spawned into world space[cite: 9]. It encapsulates collision detection filtering, dynamic damage scaling, frame-throttled impact audio compilation, debris fracture separation, and structural cleanup[cite: 9]. Custom projectile archetypes inherit directly from this script and override the OnImpact() routine to append distinct behaviors[cite: 9].

The base script requires a Rigidbody and a minimum of two AudioSource components on the root object layer[cite: 9]. The primary audio source handles frame-gated impact sounds; the secondary audio source executes looping aerodynamic properties (e.g., wind displacement whistling)[cite: 9].

The Dual-Layer Audio Paradigm: Core Projectile Sounds vs. Surface Sweeteners

To preserve memory and streamline assembly configurations, impact audio is divided into two operational segments[cite: 9]:
  • The Projectile Audio Slot (Base Class): Located on the core projectile prefabs (Boulder, Cannonball, Ballista Bolt)[cite: 9]. This pool represents the universal kinetic energy, mass, and weight of the projectile itself (e.g., a heavy sub-bass thud or metallic displacement crack)[cite: 9]. This profile remains constant regardless of what object is hit because the mass of the projectile does not change[cite: 9].
  • The Surface Impact Audio Slot (Modular Extension Component): Handled contextually by the DPT_ProjectileSurfaceImpact companion component[cite: 9]. This represents the high-frequency debris layer or environmental reaction (e.g., splintering wood fibers, shattering rock shards, or loose dirt explosions) mapped exclusively to specific target physics layers[cite: 9].

By default, this calls Destroy(), but advanced developers can override the Despawn() method to intercept this call and return the projectile to a custom Object Pool instead[cite: 9].
Despawn Delay The tracking timeframe buffer (in seconds) to wait after an impact before completely deleting the object tree from memory, allowing audio trailing or VFX particle systems to complete natively[cite: 9].
Enable Inaccuracy If true, injects both a randomized launch spread angle transformation and a continuous aerodynamic side-drift physics update using Perlin noise loops[cite: 9].

Continuous Drift Force The lateral acceleration force scalar applied continuously to push the projectile off-course, simulating micro-shifting crosswinds[cite: 9].

Embedded Debris Root A pre-fractured child container asset model configured with loose individual rigidbodies[cite: 9]. Activated, unparented, and thrown into root world space on impact[cite: 9]. Should be deactivated by default in the prefab[cite: 9].

Extending the Framework

To implement alternative ammunition varieties, author a child script that inherits directly from DPT_Projectile and override the OnImpact(Collision collision, float speed) routine[cite: 9]. The underlying engine automatically handles structural damage, frame-gated sound mixers, and debris fracturing before executing your custom override logic[cite: 9].

using UnityEngine;
using DPT.SiegeWeapons;

public class MortarShellProjectile : DPT_Projectile
{
    protected override void OnImpact(Collision collision, float speed)
    {
        // Custom area-of-effect calculations, ground deformation, or unique damage interactions
        // Core audio, structural damage, and debris logic are pre-processed by the base script
    }
}
Linking Communication Back to Weapons:

Every projectile retains an explicit reference to the siege weapon assembly that initialized it via the public Launcher property, configured automatically on spawn[cite: 9]. You can invoke Launcher.ImpactEffect(contactPoint) inside your custom overrides to trigger the parent weapon's specialized impact audio behaviors at the hit coordinate[cite: 9].
Advanced Architecture: Object Pooling Compatibility & Caching

To support massive scale sieges and strict hardware performance limits, the DPT_Projectile framework is strictly Pool-Ready and designed for zero runtime allocation[cite: 9].

During Awake(), the projectile permanently caches all internal Rigidbody and Collider arrays[cite: 9]. When a projectile shatters, it safely unparents its debris rigidbodies to prevent them from inheriting the tumbling momentum of moving targets[cite: 9].

To implement an Object Pool, subscribe your pool manager to the projectile's OnDespawnRequested Action delegate[cite: 9]. The projectile will hand itself over to your script instead of calling Destroy()[cite: 9]. When you are ready to fire it again, call ResetForPooling() to instantly vacuum up its broken debris fragments, freeze their physics, and re-enable the root colliders, making the ammunition pristine without triggering Garbage Collection spikes[cite: 9].

2. DPT_Cannonball — Standard Ballistic Ammunition

DPT_Cannonball is a specialized extension designed for heavy, high-velocity projectile assets[cite: 9]. It tracks high-speed ballistic pathways, layering a custom high-frequency pitch modification over the secondary looping audio source to simulate rapid air displacement[cite: 9]. This looping wind shear volume seamlessly moves towards an audible zero upon hitting geometry to cleanly blend into the base impact framework[cite: 9].

3. DPT_Boulder — Kinetic Rolling Projectile

DPT_Boulder alters conventional projectile lifecycle mechanics by transitioning seamlessly from a free-flying air tracking object into a grounded physics asset[cite: 9]. Rather than relying on heavy OnCollisionStay loops, it utilizes a highly performant collision contact-counter to track its grounded state[cite: 9]. This prevents micro-bouncing audio artifacts and dynamically shifts audio priorities from flying wind whistles into heavy ground friction rumbling loops that match the boulder's active velocity magnitude[cite: 9].

Inspector Property Fields

Roll Volume Smoothing The interpolation factor governing how smoothly the rolling sound loop adjusts to sudden changes in velocity or unexpected terrain drop-offs[cite: 9]. Default: 3[cite: 9].
Max Roll Speed The velocity threshold (m/s) at which the ground rolling audio loop reaches its maximum pitch shift and volume scaling boundaries[cite: 9]. Default: 15 m/s[cite: 9].
Settled Threshold The physics velocity floor below which the boulder is considered fully stationary post-impact, terminating its rolling phase[cite: 9]. Default: 0.2 m/s[cite: 9].

Rolling VFX The dust particle system driven during the rolling phase[cite: 9]. Its emission rate is dynamically linked to the boulder's physical speed multiplier[cite: 9].
Max Emission Rate The maximum number of particles to emit per second when the boulder is traveling at or above the maximum roll speed threshold[cite: 9]. Default: 20[cite: 9].

Mechanical Profile

  • Lifecycle Extension: Unhooks completely from early base class despawning chains inside Awake()[cite: 9]. It explicitly forces its internal shouldDespawn state to false on initialization, ensuring the lifespan cleanup counters do not begin processing until the rock completely finishes its rolling phase and comes to a full stop[cite: 9].
  • Dynamic Pitch Calculations: Linearly interpolates the rolling loop audio player's pitch shifting baseline between 0.8f and 1.2f based on its current kinetic velocity tracking data[cite: 9].
  • Audio-Visual Velocity Sync: The script shares a single calculated multiplier (0.0 to 1.0) derived from the Rigidbody's velocity magnitude to drive both the looping friction audio and the dust particle emission rate simultaneously[cite: 9]. This guarantees that visual dust trails smoothly taper off to zero precisely as the heavy rumbling audio fades out, preventing abrupt particle cutoffs before the boulder has fully settled[cite: 9].

4. DPT_BallistaBolt — Specialized Piercing Bolt

DPT_BallistaBolt delivers specialized features for heavy piercing ammunition assets[cite: 9]. It continuously slerps its physical rigidbody alignment to look directly into its velocity vector during flight, processes advanced multi-axis aerodynamic cosmetic vibrations, handles penetration pinning depth, and includes a structural snap mechanic that separates the tail fletching from the embedded tip on structural clipping[cite: 9].

Embed On Impact & Break On Impact Are Prefab Defaults, Not Fixed Behavior:

Unlike earlier internal builds, DPT_BallistaBolt no longer forces the inherited Embed On Impact and Break On Impact fields at runtime[cite: 9]. Whatever you configure on the prefab's Inspector is respected as-is, so the same script can be repurposed for non-embedding ammunition (foam darts, blunt bolts, practice rounds) simply by unchecking Embed On Impact — no code changes required[cite: 9].

The recommended factory defaults for a standard piercing bolt are Embed On Impact = true and Break On Impact = false, authored directly on the prefab[cite: 9].

The tail-snap mechanic (Snap Trigger Collider, Snapped Visual) is only armed when the bolt has actually embedded[cite: 9]. If Embed On Impact is disabled on a given prefab, the snap system automatically stays dormant rather than throwing errors — so it's safe to toggle off for non-piercing ammo variants[cite: 9].

Mechanical Profile

  • Inaccuracy Integration: Integrates natively with the base class aerodynamic drift mechanics[cite: 9].   • Matrix-Free Target Tracking: Utilizes the base class's native embedding architecture to bypass standard Unity transform.SetParent workflows on impact[cite: 9]. By recording its exact relative position and rotation offsets using inverse transform mapping, it completely immunizes the bolt mesh from non-uniform scaling, stretching, or skewing distortions present on target structures[cite: 9].
  • Debris Reparenting Fallback: Overrides the base class's standard unstick routine[cite: 9]. If the target object the bolt is pinned into gets broken or fractured, the bolt searches the parent weapon assembly's brokenParents arrays, finds the closest active debris piece, and updates its tracking anchors to tumble realistically with the wreckage rather than just falling out[cite: 9].

5. Modular Ballistic Extension Components

To preserve maximum performance optimization and maintain a highly scannable workflow, modular behaviors like environment visual matching and camera shaking are separated into independent, optional satellite scripts[cite: 9]. To implement these behaviors, drop these components onto any projectile prefab alongside the main baseline DPT_Projectile component tree[cite: 9].

A. DPT_ProjectileScreenShake

Triggers a decoupled, system-wide broadcast of camera shockwave metrics upon a valid ammunition impact[cite: 9]. Instead of scanning scene physics matrices using high-overhead loop parameters or heavy overlap sphere allocation checks, it handles registration through a modern, high-performance static C# event handler model[cite: 9].

Component Properties

Property Description
Shake Magnitude The peak intensity and baseline movement strength multiplier applied to listening camera rigs at the moment of impact[cite: 9].
Shake Duration The total lifespan runtime window (in seconds) over which the camera shaking displacement operates before returning to rest[cite: 9].
Shake Radius The maximum calculation distance outward from the impact epicenter where a player's viewport can be located to register the physical rumble attenuation[cite: 9].

Setup & Integration Workflow

  1. Attach to Prefab: Drag and drop the DPT_ProjectileScreenShake script onto the root GameObject of your projectile prefab[cite: 9]. Because of the [RequireComponent] attribute, it will safely verify or automatically append the base projectile logic[cite: 9].
  2. Configure Metrics: Set the baseline spatial parameters in the inspector fields[cite: 9]. For example, a heavy catapult boulder might use a magnitude of 1.2, a duration of 0.5s, and a radius of 25m, whereas a light ballista bolt may drop to 0.3 magnitude and an 8m radius[cite: 9].
  3. Hooking into Your Camera System: This module does not manipulate cameras directly; it broadcasts coordinates to the global OnProjectileImpactShake static event to remain completely decoupled[cite: 9]. To listen to this data instantly without manual scripting, drop the companion DPT_CameraShakeReceiver utility component directly onto your camera setup (see section below)[cite: 9].

B. DPT_CameraShakeReceiver (Utility)

A zero-configuration, production-ready utility script designed to serve as the native receiver for your ballistic screen shake systems[cite: 9]. It automatically tracks the impact broadcast channel, runs fast proximity distance checks, and applies linear spatial dampening to ensure close-range impacts feel appropriately devastating while distant rumbles drop smoothly to zero[cite: 9].

Component Properties

No-Configuration Workflow: This script contains no exposed serialized inspector fields[cite: 9]. It calculates all positional offsets dynamically based on the camera's baseline transform state at runtime, allowing a true drop-in implementation[cite: 9].

Setup & Integration Workflow

  1. Attach to Camera: Drag and drop the DPT_CameraShakeReceiver script directly onto your active Unity Camera GameObject[cite: 9].
  2. Cinematic Rigs & Nested Compatibility: The component manipulates transform.localPosition rather than world space coordinates[cite: 9]. This guarantees it can be applied safely to cameras nested inside complex player tracking assemblies, third-person spring arms, or cutscene rigs without breaking their structural pathing[cite: 9].

C. DPT_ProjectileSurfaceImpact

Provides robust, contextual material variations by analyzing the exact physics layers involved in the collision[cite: 9]. This enables ammunition types to dynamically map dirt, stone, wood, or water elements to distinct visual effects, audio arrays, and physical vertex-mesh surface marks (cracks, molehills, or trenches)[cite: 9].

VFX & Surface Mark Orientation Rules:

The module extracts the exact face normal vector from the structural contact matrix[cite: 9]. To ensure your impact particles spray outward correctly and your directional molehills align with the travel path, ensure that the primary directional flow of your prefab travels down its local positive Z-Axis (Forward)[cite: 9].

This orientation also matters for rolling trenches specifically: the automatic spacing calculation measures each trench mark's length along its local Z-axis to determine overlap[cite: 9]. A trench mesh authored sideways (long axis on X) will produce inaccurate spacing[cite: 9].

  1. Attach Component: Mount the DPT_ProjectileSurfaceImpact component to the root layer of your projectile prefab alongside your core collision modules[cite: 9].
  2. Assign Fallback Assets: Provide generic assets into both the Default Break VFX Prefab and Default Impact Audio Clips slots[cite: 9].   3. Populate Layer Specific Maps: Expand the list array[cite: 9]. Add unique elements for each distinct surface target archetype[cite: 9]:
    • LayerMask: Select your target material layer (e.g., Layer 10: Environment_Wood) or leave as Default if relying strictly on surface orientation[cite: 9].
    • Orientation Filter: Choose whether this effect should only trigger on flat ground (Horizontal Only), sheer walls (Vertical Only), or Any surface[cite: 9].
    • Vfx Prefab: Assign your customized debris particle spray prefab[cite: 9].
    • Surface Audio Clips: Assign your specialized high-frequency material splinter or impact sounds[cite: 9]. When the projectile hits this layer, it will combine your core baseline weapon projectile thud with these material-specific debris sounds[cite: 9].
VFX Orientation & Prefab Setup Rules:

The module extracts the exact face normal vector from the structural contact matrix using collision.GetContact(0).normal and builds a dynamic forward look rotation via Quaternion.LookRotation[cite: 9].

To ensure your impact particles spray outward correctly away from walls and floors rather than clipping directly into the solid collision geometry, ensure that the primary directional burst of your particle prefab travels down its local positive Z-Axis (Forward)[cite: 9].

6. Troubleshooting Notes

"Projectiles and broken environmental blocks are flying off too far upon impact"
This is a mass balancing issue[cite: 9]. By default, Unity initializes new rigidbodies with a mass of 1 (1 kg)[cite: 9]. Ensure your destructible blocks or brick assets have realistic masses configured (e.g., 25 to 75), and increase their Linear Drag properties to 0.5 or 1.0 to introduce realistic friction and air resistance[cite: 9].
"Ballista bolts stretch or skew into weird proportions when pinning into walls"
This occurs if an object in your scene uses non-uniform scaling values (e.g., Scale X: 3, Y: 0.5, Z: 1)[cite: 9]. Ensure your bolt prefabs utilize matrix-free tracking (using InverseTransformPoint and InverseTransformDirection parameters) to calculate coordinate offsets manually instead of physically parenting via the engine hierarchy tree[cite: 9].
